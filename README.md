## _What do we think when we listen to music?_
### °‧ 𓆝 𓆟 𓆞 ·｡

"From disco floors to deep space, jazz to jellyfish, shadows to spinning colours; looking at participants' free-response descriptions eye-to-eye highlights the multi-faceted nature of music-influenced mental content (MIMC). 

  Extending beyond traditional isolated examinations of VMI, memories, and narratives, our open and unified probing of mental content—combined with mixed-method analyses—captures structural relations between thought types that have remained largely uncharted.   

  Human‑driven qualitative reading can attend to human-nuance that computational NLP approaches routinely flatten, enabling our analysis to identify the finer connective tissue between thought types. 

  The resulting thematic framework maps a comprehensive typography of listeners' music influenced "thoughtscapes" (van der Walle et al., 2025), offering a more nuanced tapestry of imagination in action than previously captured by quantitative or computational approaches."

  —Hazel A. van der Walle [_Mar.2026_]

---

_Music‑Influenced Mental Content (MIMC)_ is defined as: 

any mental content people describe as being shaped by, influenced by, attributed to, or associated with music stimuli and its listening context, without requiring an explicit causal claim (e.g. “evoked”, “induced”). 

Framed this way, MIMC captures a heterogeneous range of multimodal experiences and unfolding scenes, spanning visual mental imagery (VMI), imagined narratives, memories, and emotional or musical appraisals.

---

Rigorous, mixed-method qualitative analysis was conducted on 14,099 MIMC descriptions, involving: 
- multiple coding passes,
- multi-coder reliability checks,
- thematic analysis, and
- co-occurrence network analysis.

The resulting thematic framework comprehensively maps the "thoughtscapes" influenced by music and highlights the multi-faceted nature of MIMC. 

My hope is that these insights can lay the groundwork for future MIMC research considering the full spectrum and typology of listeners' imaginative experiences.

### 📂 `codebook/`
The codebook documents the full coding scheme developed across the qualitative analysis. Contains three PDFs:
- **`CODEBOOK-table-landscape.pdf`** — landscape-format version of the complete codebook
- **`CODEBOOK-table-portrait.pdf`** — portrait-format version of the complete codebook
- **`CODEBOOK-table-breakdown.pdf`** — a breakdown of the full codebook, organised by theme and code category, including definitions and examples of each category.

### 📂 `data/`
The core dataset and descriptive analysis. Contains:
- **`MIMCdata.csv`** — the cleaned study dataset
- **`MIMC-data-descriptives.Rmd`** — R Markdown file for descriptive statistics and visualisation
- **`MIMC-data-descriptives.html`** — rendered output of the descriptives analysis

### 📂 `stimuli/`
Information about the music stimuli used in the study. Contains:
- **`MIMC-120clips/`** — folder housing the 120 music clips stimuli, covering 10 genres
- **`stimuli-list.csv`** — structured list of all stimuli with metadata
- **`stimuli-M17-edit-list`** — information on the edited M17 clips

### 📂 `trail/`
The auditable decision trail documenting the analytical process across all coding stages. Contains:
- **`auditable decision trail.pdf`** — record of multicoder meeting notes and decisions made throughout the study
- **`stage1-codebook.pdf`** — codebook as it stood at Stage 1 of analysis
- **`stage1-invivo.nvpx`** — NVivo project file for Stage 1 (in vivo coding)
